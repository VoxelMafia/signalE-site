Where it comes from

Which levers save the energy

Illustrative, laptop-weighted. The mix shifts by device and state — on desktops, EcoQoS and the power-mode overlay dominate and the display slice shrinks toward zero. Carbon-aware scheduling saves carbon, not kWh, so it's tracked separately.

Carbon-aware timing

The same kilowatt-hour, drawn at a cleaner hour

Grid carbon intensity swings 3–5× across a day. SignalE green-lights deferrable background work — updates, backups, sync, indexing — during clean hours and holds it back during the dirty evening peak. Below: a representative 24-hour marginal-intensity forecast, tiered by SignalE.

Green — run deferrable work now Neutral — hold Red — defer / throttle harder
Shifting deferrable load from the dirtiest to the cleanest hours cuts that slice's emissions by ~53% (428 → 200 gCO₂/kWh) — with no loss of energy and nothing the user is waiting on.
Two effects, counted separately

Why we never double-count

01 Energy reduction

EcoQoS, the power-mode overlay, idle throttling and adaptive brightness make the machine draw fewer watts for the same experience. This removes energy from the grid entirely.

→ measured live via battery discharge rate

02 Carbon-aware shift

The same deferrable kilowatt-hour, moved out of the dirty peak into a clean hour. It doesn't cut energy — it cuts the emissions of energy that was going to be spent anyway.

→ marginal grid intensity, per region
Measurement integrity

Rules that keep this out of greenwashing

Inflated climate claims are worse than none. Every number here obeys six rules — the same discipline credible carbon accounting requires.

  • ✓Additionality. Only energy that would otherwise be drawn is counted, via a per-device causal A/B baseline — not an assumed one.
  • ✓Measured beats modeled. On battery, savings are measured from real discharge rate; where only modeling is possible it's labeled as such, with wider bands.
  • ✓Net of overhead. SignalE's own energy cost is always subtracted before anything is claimed.
  • ✓No peak-as-average. Global figures use time-weighted average savings (~7–10%), never the 10–40% context peaks.
  • ✓Privacy-preserving fleet data. Opt-in, aggregated on-device with differential privacy — region and totals only, never app or usage detail.
  • ✓Published method. Open model, every input an editable range, outputs as distributions with visible uncertainty.
